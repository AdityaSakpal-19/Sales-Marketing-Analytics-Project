## Sales & Marketing Performance Analytics

### Business Objective
Analyze sales and marketing performance to identify revenue drivers, product concentration risks, and channel optimization opportunities.

### Tools Used
- SQL: Data aggregation and KPI calculations
- Excel: Validation and exploratory analysis
- Power BI: Interactive dashboards and executive reporting

### Analytical Approach
- Performed data validation to ensure accuracy and consistency across sales metrics.
- Defined and tracked key KPIs including revenue, AOV, order volume, units sold, and MoM growth.
- Analyzed product and category performance to identify Pareto (80/20) revenue patterns.
- Evaluated regional sales distribution to assess concentration risk and growth balance.
- Analyzed acquisition channels to compare revenue contribution and efficiency across Partner, Organic, Referral, and Paid Ads.
- Assessed discount impact to identify categories with higher promotional dependency.

### Key Insights
- Revenue shows month-to-month volatility, indicating sensitivity to seasonality and promotions rather than steady organic growth.
- Top-performing products contribute a disproportionate share of total revenue, confirming strong Pareto concentration.
- Office Supplies leads in revenue and volume, while Electronics and Furniture show lower contribution.
- Partner and Organic channels drive the highest revenue, while Paid Ads underperform, indicating potential ROI inefficiencies.
- Corporate customers generate higher average revenue per customer despite lower volume.

### Business Recommendations
- Prioritize inventory and pricing discipline for high-contribution products.
- Review discount strategies for underperforming categories to reduce margin leakage.
- Reallocate marketing spend toward Partner and Organic channels.
- Develop targeted initiatives to grow high-value Corporate customer segments.
